About Palau, Micronesia

Palau is a small island country located in the western Pacific Ocean, east of the Philippines. It consists of over 300 islands, but only eight of them are inhabited. Palau is known for its pristine beaches, crystal clear waters, and coral reefs that are home to an abundance of marine life.  

The population is around 18,000 people and the official languages are Palauan and English. As the country is very much modernized today, the people still follow cultural heritage through traditional dances, crafts, and architecture.

Palau is a popular tourist destination for activities such as snorkeling, scuba diving, kayaking, and simply relaxing. Foreigners who plan to visit are required to sign a pledge that encourage people to act responsibly and sustainably during the visit, and to support efforts to protect the environment.
